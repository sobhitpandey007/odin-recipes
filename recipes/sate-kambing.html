sate-kambing

description

Sate Kambing is a traditional Indonesian dish made with tender pieces of goat meat marinated in flavorful spices, skewered, and grilled over hot charcoal. It is known for its smoky aroma, juicy texture, and rich taste.

The satay is often served with a sweet soy sauce mixed with sliced shallots and chilies or with peanut sauce, along with steamed rice or rice cakes. It is a popular choice for family gatherings, celebrations, and street food lovers.

ingredients

  • 500g goat meat, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water)
  • Sliced shallots, for serving
  • Sliced red chilies, for serving
  • Peanut sauce or extra sweet soy sauce (optional)

steps

  1. Cut the goat meat into small, bite-sized cubes.
  2. In a bowl, combine the sweet soy sauce, vegetable oil, garlic, ground coriander, black pepper, salt, and lime juice.
  3. Add the goat meat to the marinade and mix well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
  5. Preheat a grill or charcoal barbecue to medium-high heat.
  6. Grill the skewers for 8–10 minutes, turning them occasionally and brushing with extra marinade until the meat is cooked and lightly charred.
  7. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
  8. Serve the Sate Kambing with sliced shallots, sliced red chilies, sweet soy sauce or peanut sauce, and steamed rice or rice cakes.
